To maximize SEO visibility in Google Search, create high-quality, people-first content that fulfills user needs, optimize for technical speed and mobile-friendliness, and ensure content is easily crawlable. Key actions include using descriptive URLs, structuring content with headers (H1-H3), using descriptive alt text for images, and earning reputable backlinks.

Core Guidelines for SEO Visibility
Create Helpful Content: Focus on creating unique, high-quality, and reliable content designed to help users rather than to manipulate search engines. Content should be comprehensive, relevant, and answer specific user queries.
Keyword Optimization & Strategy:
Informational Keywords: Target these in blog posts.
Product/Commercial Keywords: Include on product or collection pages.
Placement: Place keywords naturally in titles, headers (H1, H2), and throughout the content.
Website Structure & Technical SEO:
URLs: Use simple, descriptive URLs that reflect page content.
Internal Linking: Connect relevant pages to improve navigation and search engine crawling.
Mobile-Friendliness: Ensure your site operates perfectly on mobile devices.
Site Speed: Optimize page load times to meet Core Web Vitals standards.
On-Page SEO Best Practices:
Title Tags & Meta Descriptions: Write unique, compelling titles and descriptions that encourage clicks.
Image Optimization: Use descriptive filenames and informative alt text (e.g., golden-retriever-puppy.jpg instead of IMG001.jpg).
Earning Authority & Trust:
Backlinks: Earn high-quality links from reputable websites to improve domain authority.
Author Credibility: Link content to knowledgeable authors.
Local SEO (If Applicable):
Google Business Profile: Keep business information, such as address, phone number, and hours, accurate and complete to improve local visibility.

Things to Avoid
Keyword Stuffing: Avoid filling content with keywords, which provides a poor user experience.
Duplicate Content: Reduce or eliminate duplicate content across your site.
Distracting Ads: Avoid excessive or distracting advertisements that interfere with the user experience.
